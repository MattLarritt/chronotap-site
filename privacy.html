Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 July 2026

ChronoTap is built to respect your privacy. This policy explains what the app does — and, more importantly, doesn't do — with your information.

The short version

ChronoTap does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any of your personal data. The developer never sees your activities, sessions, notes, or times.

What ChronoTap stores

The activities (tags) and time sessions you create are stored:

  • On your device, so the app works fully offline; and
  • In your own private iCloud account (Apple's CloudKit private database), if you are signed in to iCloud, so your data syncs across your own devices.

This iCloud data lives in your personal iCloud storage and is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. The developer of ChronoTap has no access to it.

What ChronoTap does NOT do

  • No account or sign-up — ChronoTap has no login.
  • No analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking.
  • No advertising and no advertising identifiers.
  • No third-party SDKs that collect data.
  • No selling or sharing of data with anyone.

Notifications

If you enable notifications, ChronoTap schedules reminders (for example, to review a recorded session) locally on your device. No notification content is sent to any server.

Data export

You can export your sessions to a CSV file at any time and share it using the standard iOS share sheet. Where that file goes after you share it is your choice and outside the app's control.

Children's privacy

ChronoTap does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date.
